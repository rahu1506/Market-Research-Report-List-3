<p><p>Vertical microscopes are designed with the objectives located above the stage, allowing for easy access and manipulation of samples. These microscopes are ideal for observing thick samples or specimens that require a larger working distance. Inverted microscopes, on the other hand, have the objectives located below the stage, making them ideal for observing living cells or organisms in liquid medium. These microscopes are commonly used in cell culture and fluorescence microscopy applications. Both types offer unique advantages depending on the specific needs of the user.</p></p>
