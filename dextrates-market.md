<p><p>Dextrates are commonly used as excipients in pharmaceutical formulations. Anhydrous Dextrates are typically used in dry powder formulations, as they contain no water molecules. Hydrous Dextrates, on the other hand, contain water molecules and are often used in wet granulation processes. Both types of Dextrates cater to different market needs based on the formulation requirements of pharmaceutical products. Anhydrous Dextrates offer stability and flow properties, while Hydrous Dextrates provide binding and disintegration properties in formulations.</p></p>
