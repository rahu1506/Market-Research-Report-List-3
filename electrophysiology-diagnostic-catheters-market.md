<p><p>Electrophysiology diagnostic catheters can be categorized into imaging catheters and non-imaging catheters. Imaging catheters are equipped with technology that allows for visualization inside the body during procedures, providing real-time images to assist in diagnosis and treatment. Non-imaging catheters, on the other hand, do not have imaging capabilities but are used to deliver therapy or collect data during electrophysiology procedures. Both types of catheters play important roles in diagnosing and treating various cardiac conditions by guiding the cardiologist during procedures.</p></p>
