<p><p>Corn flour is commonly categorized into different market types based on specific characteristics. Sweet degermed corn flour, which is made from corn kernels with the germ removed, is often used in baking and cooking for its mild flavor and fine texture. Waxy degermed corn flour, on the other hand, contains a higher percentage of amylopectin and is preferred for its thickening properties. Other market types of corn flour may include specialty or organic varieties that cater to niche consumer preferences.</p></p>
