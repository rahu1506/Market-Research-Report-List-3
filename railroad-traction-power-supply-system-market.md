<p><p>Railroad traction power supply systems can be broadly categorized into two types: AC electrification systems and DC electrification systems. AC electrification systems use alternating current to power trains, while DC electrification systems use direct current. Each type has its own advantages and applications, with AC systems typically being more suitable for high-speed trains and long-distance travel, while DC systems are often used for urban transit and commuter rail services. Both systems play a crucial role in providing efficient and sustainable power to railway networks.</p></p>
