<p><p>Disk brakes are commonly categorized into two main types - Opposed Piston Type and Floating Caliper Type. In Opposed Piston Type, the brake caliper has pistons on both sides of the rotor, which apply pressure evenly for more consistent braking performance. Floating Caliper Type, on the other hand, uses a single piston that moves within the caliper to press the brake pads against the rotor. Both types have their own advantages and are widely used in the automotive industry.</p></p>
