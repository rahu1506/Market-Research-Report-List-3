<p><p>Heat transfer fluids play a crucial role in semiconductor processes by efficiently transferring heat away from components. Two common types of heat transfer fluids used in the industry are PFAS (perfluoroalkyl substances) and PFPE (perfluoropolyether). PFAS fluids offer high thermal stability and are commonly used in high-temperature applications. On the other hand, PFPE fluids provide excellent chemical resistance and are often used in processes involving harsh chemicals. Both types of fluids help ensure optimal heat dissipation and protect sensitive semiconductor components during manufacturing.</p></p>
