<p><strong>The Non-wearable Sleep Monitoring Devices Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Contact</li><li>Contactless</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Contact sleep monitoring devices require physical contact with the body to track sleep patterns, such as wristbands or headbands. Contactless sleep monitoring devices, on the other hand, use sensors that do not require direct contact with the body, such as bedside monitors or under-mattress sensors. Both types of devices provide valuable insights into sleep quality, duration, and patterns, but contactless devices offer a more convenient and non-intrusive solution for individuals who prefer not to wear a tracking device while sleeping.</p></p>
