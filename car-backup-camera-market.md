<p><p>CCD (charge-coupled device) cameras and CMOS (complementary metal-oxide-semiconductor) cameras are the two main types of technologies used in car backup cameras. CCD cameras provide higher image quality and better performance in low light conditions, making them the preferred choice for professional use. On the other hand, CMOS cameras are more cost-effective and energy-efficient, making them popular in consumer-grade backup camera systems. Both types offer reliable rearview monitoring to enhance driver safety and convenience.</p></p>
